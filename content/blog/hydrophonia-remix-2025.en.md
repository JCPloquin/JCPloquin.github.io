# Hydrophonia - remix (2025)  
*Aquatic poem and Anthropocene crisis*

## Overview

*Hydrophonia - remix (2025)* is an electroacoustic poem tracing the journey of water in all its forms, **from its cosmic emergence to the catastrophe of the Anthropocene**. The piece explores:

- the genesis of water,
- its secret flows,
- its metamorphoses,
- its real and mythical landscapes,
- up to the contamination and destruction caused by modern humanity.

This project offers an **immersive listening experience of our contemporary ecological crisis**, through the lens of the primordial element—water.

## Genesis and Versions

This work was **originally composed for octophonic diffusion**, tailored to the acousmonium setup of the Apnées festival (PAYSAGES | COMPOSÉS 2025), allowing full spatialization on eight speakers.  
*The version presented here is a stereophonic reduction, specially adapted for YouTube.*  
**Headphone listening is strongly recommended** to fully experience the richness and inner movement of the sound spaces.

## Context and Current Status

This composition was **submitted for the [PAYSAGES | COMPOSÉS 2025 call for works](https://apnees.wordpress.com/2025/06/04/appel-a-oeuvres-paysages-composes-2025/)**, organized by Apnées.  
*Selection decision pending.*

## A Sonic and Poetic Journey

*Hydrophonia - remix* invites the listener on a sonic journey:  
*A mirror for both our mythologies and our excesses, water transforms from a primordial flow and force of life into a victim of human activity in the age of the Anthropocene.*

## A Unique Visual Perspective: Bruno Maurey

The images in this video are drawn from **unreleased footage provided by Bruno Maurey**, a photographer and video artist whose visual poetry explores:

> "light, slowness, the boundary between radiance and fading."

His search for the *"passage of time within the image"* resonates naturally with the aquatic, ever-shifting narrative of the piece.

> "Capturing light, casting slowness into reality."

Thank you to Bruno Maurey for this rare visual material, an invitation to:

> "explore what resists, trembles, or dissolves" —  
> an alchemy of image and sound.
